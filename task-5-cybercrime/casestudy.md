Case Study: The "Reverse-QR" UPI Fraud
Definition of the Crime
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) Fraud is a form of financial cybercrime that leverages social engineering to deceive victims into authorizing unauthorized debits from their bank accounts. The most prevalent variant is the "Reverse-QR Code" scam, where the attacker tricks the victim into believing they are receiving money, when they are actually digitally signing a "Pay" instruction.

The Step-by-Step Execution (Fictional Scenario)
The Hook: A college student, "Aryan," posts an ad for a used DSLR camera on a digital marketplace like OLX.

The Contact: Within minutes, a "buyer" calls Aryan, claiming to be an Army officer stationed nearby. He agrees to the full price immediately to build trust and bypass negotiation.

The Bait: The buyer insists on paying a "token amount" via WhatsApp to secure the deal. He sends a QR code image to Aryan.

The Deception: The buyer stays on the call, creating a sense of urgency. He instructs Aryan: "I have sent the payment. Just scan this code in your GPay and enter your PIN to 'claim' the money into your account."

The Theft: Aryan, distracted and rushed, scans the code and enters his UPI PIN. Since a PIN is only required to send money, the app instantly debits ₹15,000 from Aryan’s account and transfers it to the scammer.

Target Demographic
The primary targets are students and young professionals engaged in the "gig economy" or second-hand marketplaces. They are targeted because they are digitally active, frequently use UPI for small transactions, and are often eager for quick sales or part-time income.

Consequences
The immediate impact is total financial loss of the account balance. Beyond the money, victims face emotional trauma, anxiety, and the logistical nightmare of freezing bank accounts and filing police reports. For a student, this loss often represents months of savings or subsistence allowance.
