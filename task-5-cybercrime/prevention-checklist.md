Stay Safe Online: A Prevention Checklist for Students
This checklist is designed to help you navigate the digital landscape securely while balancing academics, social life, and finances.

 General Digital Hygiene
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on your university portals, Gmail, and social media accounts. Use an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) instead of SMS codes whenever possible to prevent SIM-swapping attacks.

Secure Your Webcam and Mic: Use a physical slider or a piece of tape to cover your laptop camera when not in use. Be mindful of which apps have "Always On" permissions for your microphone in your phone settings.

Vulnerability Management: Regularly update your OS (Windows/macOS/Android/iOS) and browsers. These updates often contain critical security patches for "Zero-Day" vulnerabilities that hackers exploit.

Public Wi-Fi Caution: Avoid accessing sensitive accounts (like your student portal or bank) over open campus or café Wi-Fi. If you must use public networks, always use a reputable VPN to encrypt your data.

 Financial & UPI Safety
The Golden Rule of UPI: Remember that entering your UPI PIN is only for sending money, never for receiving it. If someone asks you to "scan a QR code to receive a refund or payment," it is a scam.

Set Daily Transaction Limits: Use your banking or UPI app (BHIM, GPay, PhonePe) to set a low daily transaction limit. This minimizes potential loss if your device is compromised or if you are coerced into a fraudulent transfer.

 Academic & Social Safety
Vet Third-Party Academic Tools: Before uploading your thesis or assignments to "Free Plagiarism Checkers" or "AI Summarizers," ensure the site is legitimate. Many of these sites exist solely to harvest your data or steal your intellectual property.

Spot the "Urgent Friend" Scam: If a classmate or friend messages you on WhatsApp/Instagram asking for an urgent transfer because of a "medical emergency" or "locked account," always verify via a direct phone call before acting.

 How to Report Cybercrime
If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital scam or online harassment, take immediate action:

National Helpline: Call 1930 immediately to report financial fraud (this increases the chances of freezing the stolen funds in the scammer's account).
